## Selected Research

**Human Judgment as Structural Constraint in AI-Mediated Complex Systems**

My research examines how human judgment, responsibility, provenance, and decision boundaries remain necessary as automated systems become faster, more distributed, and easier to replicate.

你好，我是涂绪芬。

如果一定要给自己一个身份，我大概是一个对“人为什么会这样思考”长期保持好奇的人。这种好奇并没有把我带进某一个单独的学科，而是让我不断进入真实生活中的不同系统：身份、技术、健康、教育、决策、责任，以及人在快速变化中如何保持稳定。

Hi, I’m Xufen Tu.

I am an independent interdisciplinary researcher whose work grew from a long curiosity about why people think, decide, adapt, and lose clarity inside systems that move faster than they can process.

I have lived across different countries and worked through different stages of research involving identity, access, remote control, human state, health, attention, education, responsibility, and complex systems. Over time, these experiences led me to see that decisions are never shaped by information alone. Emotion, environment, time pressure, identity, responsibility, and system structure all influence how people understand reality and move toward action.

These observations gradually converged into my current research on human judgment in AI-mediated complex systems. I study how automation changes the location of responsibility, how decision boundaries become unclear, and what must remain human when systems become faster, more distributed, and easier to replicate.

This public page is not a complete methodology or a full research archive. It is a stable public reference for my research identity, framework, publications, model overviews, and continuing development. Detailed methods, tools, validation materials, and implementation structures are maintained privately.

If you are exploring cognition, identity, AI governance, complex systems, or the question of how people can remain clear and responsible inside accelerating systems, we will probably have a meaningful conversation.
